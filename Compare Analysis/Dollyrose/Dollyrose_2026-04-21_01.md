### 5. Final Audit Conclusions:
The HPS disparity between Dollyrose and the Reference is driven by three primary technical factors: **Casting Frequency (Downtime)**, **Spell Timing (Efficiency)**, and **Talent/Cooldown Synergy**.

1.  **Downtime Gap:** Dollyrose lost roughly 16% of the fight to inactivity. In a healer-intensive encounter like Imperator Averzian, this missing throughput cannot be recovered. The Reference cast 40 more spells in the same duration.
2.  **Halo Utilization:** Halo is the cornerstone of modern Holy Priest throughput. The Reference cast Halo 4 times (every 60s), while Dollyrose only cast it twice. This resulted in a 2.4M healing deficit from a single ability.
3.  **Efficiency per Cast:** The Reference achieved nearly double the healing per cast on *Prayer of Healing* and *Holy Word: Serenity*. This suggests the Reference is better aligning these casts with high-damage phases and utilizing *Divine Image* procs more effectively. Dollyrose is frequently casting these spells into low-damage windows or failing to maximize the *Archon/Oracle* interactions that boost Holy Word value.
4.  **Defensive Neglect:** The Reference used *Fade* (likely talented for Damage Reduction) 7 times compared to the Player's 2. This suggests a higher level of mechanic awareness and mitigation, allowing more focus on throughput rather than self-preservation.

**Recommendation:** Focus on 100% uptime on *Halo* and *Prayer of Mending*. Eliminate the 38s of downtime by filling empty windows with *Smite* or *Renew* to keep the GCD moving and proc *Divine Image* consistently.
