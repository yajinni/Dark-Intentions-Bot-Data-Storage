### 7. Final Audit Conclusions:
The primary divergence in performance is driven by **Stat Optimization** and **Execute Efficiency**. 

1. **The Mastery Gap:** The Reference has prioritized Mastery significantly more than Seze (54.67% vs 20.54%). For Fury Warriors, Mastery directly increases damage dealt during Enrage. This is visible in the "Per Cast" values across almost all spells, specifically **Execute** and **Rampage**, where the Reference hits 20-30% harder per button press.
2. **Execute Execution:** The Reference cast Execute 23 times in a shorter fight duration, while Seze only managed 18 casts in 2:28. As a Slayer-spec Warrior, maximizing Execute windows is critical. The Reference's higher crit rate on Execute (47.1% vs 39.5%) also contributed to a massive total damage gap for this specific ability.
3. **Filler Prioritization:** Seze cast **Raging Blow** 21 times, whereas the Reference only cast it 9 times. The Reference prioritized higher-value abilities (Bloodthirst/Execute/Rampage) more effectively, using Raging Blow only as a last resort filler, whereas Seze relied on it more heavily, likely due to suboptimal resource generation or prioritization.
4. **Rotational Polish:** Seze's higher downtime (3.5%) indicates moments where the rotation stalled or movement forced a gap in GCD usage. Minimizing this to match the Reference's 1.2% would provide approximately 2-3 additional high-value casts per encounter.
