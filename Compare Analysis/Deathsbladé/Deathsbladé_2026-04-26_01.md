### 7. Final Audit Conclusions:
The performance gap between Deathsbladé and the Reference is primarily driven by **Resource Efficiency** and **Defensive Neglect**. 

1.  **Death Strike Frequency:** The Player cast Death Strike 10 fewer times. In Blood DK gameplay, Death Strike is the primary source of both healing and damage. This shortfall is caused by lower **Heart Strike** and **Blood Boil** usage, which are essential for Runic Power generation.
2.  **Mastery & Blood Shield:** The Reference carries +4.56% more Mastery. For a Blood DK, Mastery increases the size of the **Blood Shield** created by Death Strike. Combined with the higher cast count, the Reference is significantly more durable and generates more "Per Cast" value.
3.  **Defensive Cooldown Desync:** The most critical finding is the total absence of major defensive usage. Deathsbladé used zero charges of **Vampiric Blood**, **Icebound Fortitude**, **Lichborne**, or **Anti-Magic Zone**. The Reference utilized these abilities constantly to smooth out damage intake and increase maximum health.
4.  **Bone Shield Maintenance:** Deathsbladé cast **Marrowrend** 33% less than the Reference, resulting in lower Bone Shield uptime. Maintaining high stacks is vital for armor and Haste (via Abomination Limb/procs). 
5.  **Per Cast Variance:** The Reference has significantly higher "Per Cast" values across all abilities. This suggests the Reference is better at lining up **Reaper's Mark** and **Exterminate** windows with high-strength procs or trinket effects, whereas the Player's damage profile is relatively flat.
