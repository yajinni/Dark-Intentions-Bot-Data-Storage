### 5. Final Audit Conclusions:
The 35% DPS gap is primarily driven by **GCD Efficiency** and **Stat Optimization**, compounded by a total lack of defensive integration into the rotation.

1.  **GCD Throughput:** The Player cast 122 spells compared to the Reference's 165. For a Blood Death Knight, filling every GCD is vital for Runic Power generation. Deathsbladé's lower **Heart Strike** and **Blood Boil** counts resulted in fewer **Death Strikes** (27 vs 37), which is the primary source of both damage and survival.
2.  **Mastery and Blood Shield:** The Reference prioritizes Mastery (19.30%) over the Player (14.74%). In the Blood spec, Mastery directly increases the size of the **Blood Shield** generated by Death Strike and increases Attack Power. This explains why the Reference's "Per Cast" value for Death Strike is 20.6K higher.
3.  **Bone Shield Management:** Deathsbladé cast **Marrowrend** 33% less often than the Reference. Maintaining high stacks of Bone Shield is critical for Armor and Haste (via Abomination Limb/Talents). 
4.  **Defensive Neglect:** The most glaring issue is the Player's failure to use **Vampiric Blood**. The Reference used it 4 times in a 2:41 fight. Vampiric Blood not only increases survivability but, through various talents, increases healing received and maximum health, which scales the damage of certain abilities.
5.  **Reaper's Mark & Exterminate:** While both used Reaper's Mark 4 times, the Reference's damage per cast was nearly double. This suggests the Reference is aligning these hits with higher **Dancing Rune Weapon** uptime or strength procs, whereas the Player is casting them regardless of buff state. 
6.  **Utility:** Deathsbladé used **Death's Caress** 3 times vs the Reference's 1. Death's Caress is generally a low-priority filler used only when moving out of melee range; its high usage by the Player suggests poor positioning or rotational confusion.
