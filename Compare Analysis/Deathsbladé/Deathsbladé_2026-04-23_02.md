### 5. Final Audit Conclusions:
The performance gap between Deathsbladé and the Reference is primarily driven by three factors: rotational velocity, defensive integration, and stat distribution.

1.  **Defensive Neglect:** The most glaring issue is the complete lack of core defensive usage. Deathsbladé failed to cast **Vampiric Blood**, **Lichborne**, or **Icebound Fortitude** a single time. In contrast, the Reference used these abilities to bridge gaps in healer throughput and likely trigger secondary benefits (such as Cooldown reduction or resource generation).
2.  **Stat Inefficiency:** Deathsbladé is heavily over-invested in **Versatility** (6.22% vs 0.98%) at the expense of **Mastery** (14.74% vs 19.30%). For a Blood DK, Mastery is the primary engine for **Blood Shield** scaling. The Reference's higher Mastery not only provides a larger physical buffer but significantly increases the value of every **Death Strike**.
3.  **Rotational Throughput:** The Reference achieved 10 more **Death Strikes** and 7 more **Blood Boils**. This suggests Deathsbladé is either capping Runic Power or leaving Runes sitting idle. The "Per Cast" value for **Heart Strike** and **Death Strike** was also significantly lower for the Player, likely due to failing to pool resources for high-value windows or missing internal synergies like **Bone Shield** stack management (as evidenced by fewer **Marrowrend** casts despite the shorter duration). 
4.  **Utility/Talent Gaps:** The Reference utilized **Abomination Limb** and **Blood Fury** to increase damage and resource generation, while Deathsbladé relied on **Raise Dead**, which provides significantly less value in a tanking context on this encounter.
