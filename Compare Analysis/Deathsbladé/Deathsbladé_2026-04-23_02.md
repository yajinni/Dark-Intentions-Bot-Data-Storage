### 7. Final Audit Conclusions:
The performance gap between Deathsbladé and the Reference is driven by three key factors: **Defensive Neglect**, **Resource Efficiency**, and **Stat Optimization**.

1.  **Defensive Neglect:** The most alarming finding is the Player's complete failure to use **Vampiric Blood**. In a 2:28 encounter, this should be used at least 3-4 times to maximize health pool and healing received. Furthermore, the absence of **Icebound Fortitude**, **Lichborne**, and **Anti-Magic Zone** indicates the Player is relying entirely on passive mitigation and healers, rather than the Blood DK's active mitigation toolkit.
2.  **Resource & Bone Shield Management:** The Player cast **Marrowrend** 33% less often than the Reference. For a Blood DK, maintaining high **Bone Shield** stacks is vital for Armor and Haste (via Abominable Might/Ossuary). The Player compensated by over-casting **Death and Decay**, which provides less direct value in a single-target encounter compared to maintaining the Bone Shield economy.
3.  **Rotational Divergence:** The Reference's **Death Strike** "Per Cast" value is 34.9% higher despite similar iLvl. This points to better **Blood Shield** (Mastery) scaling and timing Death Strikes after large damage intakes to maximize the heal. The Player's **Heart Strike** frequency and damage were significantly lower, suggesting they are not effectively using the "cleave" window of Death and Decay or are failing to spend Runes efficiently to generate Runic Power.
4.  **Stat Profile:** The Player is heavily invested in **Versatility** (6.22%), whereas the Reference has prioritized **Mastery** (19.30%). Mastery is the premier scaling stat for Blood DK damage and the physical shield component of Death Strike. Shifting secondary stats from Versatility to Mastery will provide a significant boost to both DPS and survivability.
