### 7. Final Audit Conclusions:
Deathsbladé's performance is hindered by two primary factors: low GCD efficiency and negligible defensive utility usage. 

1.  **Rotational Throughput:** The Player is casting significantly less than the Reference. Specifically, **Death Strike** (27 vs 37) and **Marrowrend** (10 vs 15) counts are low, leading to reduced Runic Power spending and lower Bone Shield stability. The "Per Cast" gap in **Heart Strike** (10.6K vs 24.0K) suggests the Player is failing to consistently cast Heart Strike within **Death and Decay** for cleave/value or during high-proc windows.
2.  **Resource Management:** The lower Marrowrend count suggests Deathsbladé may be letting Bone Shield stacks dip too low, missing out on the Haste and armor benefits provided by the buff.
3.  **Defensive Neglect:** The most glaring issue is the total absence of **Vampiric Blood**, **Lichborne**, and **Icebound Fortitude**. As a Blood Death Knight, these are not just "emergency" buttons but core rotational survival and throughput tools (via HP increases and legendary/talent interactions). 
4.  **Mastery Deficit:** The 4.56% Mastery gap significantly reduces the effectiveness of the Player's **Blood Shield**, forcing more reliance on healers and reducing the passive damage scaling of physical-based abilities. To improve, Deathsbladé must prioritize filling every GCD and rotating major defensives like Vampiric Blood proactively.
